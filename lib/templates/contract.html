KENYA EMBASSY IN MUSCAT, SULTANATE OF OMAN
EMPLOYMENT CONTRACT FOR BLUE-COLLAR JOBS FROM KENYA

The Agreement is made on: {{CONTRACT_START_DATE}}
Between the First Party (Employer/Omani Agency), Second Party (Recruitment Agency), and Third Party (Employee).

A — The Above-mentioned Parties have agreed to the Following:

  1. The Third Party commits to work under the First Party's supervision and administration, or whomever they designate as their representative, for a monthly salary not less than 140 Omani Rials.
  2. The period of this Contract is for two years, starting from the date of arrival of the Third Party to Oman.
  3. The Contract is valid for two years and may be renewed with new terms and conditions with mutual agreement of both Parties. The Employer should notify the Employee of the intended renewal three months before the Contract expires.
  4. Working hours:
    • She/he shall be provided with continuous rest of at least 8 hours per day, she/he must not work for more than 10 hours a day and must be allowed no less than an hour break after 5 consecutive hours of work.
    • She/he entitled to 1 day off per week.
    • Overtime and leave pay: as per Omani Labor Law.

B — The First party undertakes and agrees to:

  1. To pay the Third Party's salary after the end of every month or corresponding to the period when it becomes due, by bank transfer.
  2. To issue an Air Travel ticket at his/her expense for the second party to Kenya in the following cases:
    1. Where the First Party decides to terminate the contract prematurely or when the Contract expires.
    2. Where the Third-Party travels to his/her Country of origin during his/her leave period.
    3. In case of death the employer will insure Repatriation of the body to Kenya.
    4. The ticket should be booked on a direct flight or the most convenient flight to the closest Airport to her hometown in Kenya.
    5. To give details of the new Sponsor in case of an intention transfer of sponsorship and present the employee to the embassy for consent.
    6. To provide medical insurance cover for the employee against occupational injuries, illnesses, death etc. as per Omani Labor Law.

C — The Second party undertakes and agrees to:

  1. To offer basic training on:
    1. the nature of work the employees should expect;
    2. Kitchen appliances and other house appliances that relate to employee's work;
    3. Cultural, religion and language difference and how to cope;
    4. Weather changes and how to adapt.
  2. Conduct medical tests as required by Oman Labour Laws. This include, HIV tests, Tuberculosis, Hepatitis B&C and Pregnant test. This will ensure that the employee is free from major illness and is not pregnant therefore capable of conducting her duties and responsibilities efficiently and effectively.
  3. To disclose all relevant information such as salary expected and not less than Omr. 140, work load expected, the number of family members to be attended to and the size of the houses where the employee will be working.
  4. Maintain contacts with the employee checking on her wellbeing while abroad.
  5. In an unlikely event of any emergency such as death of immediate family member, sickness or inability to work as required, second part to follow-up with the First Party to ensure ticketing and repatriation are done as efficiently and effectively as possible.

D — The Third Party undertakes to carry out the obligations required by his/her job, particularly the following:

  1. Exerting reasonable efforts to carry out the work, and following the instructions of the First Party as to the assigned work, unless such instructions violate laws, regulations are against personal integrity, or are in violation of this contract conditions.
  2. Keeping any information relating to the First Party confidential.
  3. Respecting the Islamic Religion, Social Traditions, Customs and Moral values and rules and regulations effective in the sultanate of Oman.
  4. Not working for any other person before completing the procedures of changing to another Employer.

E — Termination of Contract

  1. Both parties may terminate the Contract upon giving the other party 30 days' notice in writing.
  2. The First Party may terminate this Contract without serving any notice to the Third Party if there is any serious misconduct or violations of the provisions of this Contract by the Third Party.
  3. The Third Party may terminate this Contract without serving any notice to the First Party for any of this causes:
    1. If there is a serious breach of this Contract by the First Party.
    2. The Third Party suffered ill-treatment by the Employer or was treated unfairly.
    3. Abuse by the First Party.
    4. In case of work injury or illness.

F — Dispute Resolution

In case of a dispute between the two parties the Embassy takes precedence to prevail upon the matter, if the disputes unsolved the two parties have the right to request for an amicable settlement of disputes, from the Ministry of Manpower (Dispute Settlement Department) which is obligated to take the necessary steps to reach a settlement. If a settlement is reached, the department has to issue a Certificate to that effect and it shall be binding on both parties.

H — Signing the Contract

The Contract shall be drafted in both the Arabic and English languages and signed in triplicate at the Embassy of the Republic of Kenya in Muscat (A copy for each Party with one copy remaining in the Embassy). The Employer shall present the Employee to the Embassy within the first week of arrival to the Sultanate.
